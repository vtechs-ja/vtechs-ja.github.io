FinCore

A self-hosted personal finance app, built privacy-first. All your data lives in a database you control — no bank credentials stored, no third-party financial data intermediaries. Track spending, plan budgets, and take control of your financial health, simply and clearly.

What It Is

Privacy-first, by design

FinCore ships as a full web application with a PWA layer for mobile use, plus a Home Assistant add-on for anyone who wants to run it entirely on their own hardware. Bank connectivity is designed around the SimpleFIN Protocol — a read-only bank data standard — so your credentials are never stored, and there's no third-party middleman sitting on top of your financial data.

What FinCore Does

See your full financial picture

Accounts & Transactions

Track checking, savings, credit, investment, and other accounts, with categorised transactions and multi-currency support.

Budgeting

Category-based budget rules with monthly, yearly, or weekly periods, rollover, and alert thresholds when you're over.

Dashboard & Insights

Savings rate, spending velocity, budget adherence, top merchants, and category trends — the picture at a glance.

Household Workspaces

Workspace isolation with owner, write, and read roles, so a household or small team can share one financial picture.

Self-Hosted Option

A Home Assistant add-on packages FinCore for anyone who wants to run it on their own infrastructure, not ours.

Installable PWA

Installable on Android and iOS with a mobile-first bottom tab bar — no app store required.

Where Things Stand

Paused on the hardest part — automated bank data

FinCore's core promise is seeing your full financial picture without manual data entry. That depends on reliable, automated bank data — and Jamaican banks currently have no SimpleFIN support or open banking APIs to plug into. We've built a Claude-powered PDF statement import as a manual workaround, but it isn't the seamless experience the product deserves, so active development is on pause until a workable path to automated bank data emerges for the Caribbean market.

Design Decisions

Built privacy-first, on purpose

01

SimpleFIN Over Plaid

Read-only, no credential sharing. Works outside the US banking ecosystem in theory — the honest limitation today is that Jamaican bank coverage isn't there yet in practice.

02

PWA Over Native Mobile

The primary use case is online-first, so FinCore ships as an installable web app rather than a native app store download — still the right call.

03

Claude for PDF Parsing

Generic PDF libraries can't handle arbitrary bank statement layouts. Claude's vision capability reads them reliably as a fallback data path.

04

No Third-Party Financial Intermediaries

Your data lives in a database you control. Bank credentials are never stored, and no third party sits between you and your own financial picture.
