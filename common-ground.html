Common Ground

A shared financial planning app for couples. Both partners see the same picture — income, spending, assets, liabilities, and goals — with a monthly check-in to stay aligned and build toward shared milestones together.

The Problem

Most couples don't lack money — they lack alignment

Traditional expense trackers require daily data entry and bank API integrations that are unavailable or unreliable in markets like Jamaica. There's no lightweight tool designed to keep household partners on the same financial page at a high level — focused on goals, commitments, and periodic check-ins rather than transaction-by-transaction tracking.

Two deeper problems compound it: most tools only model fixed monthly costs, ignoring irregular expenses like car servicing or back-to-school costs — so a household looks like it has a surplus but consistently feels like it doesn't. And without a record over time, couples can't answer "are we making progress?" or "when will we reach our goal?"

How It Works

Three pillars, one shared picture

The value is in communication and accountability, not data automation. Input is intentional and infrequent — the value comes from shared understanding, not transaction accuracy.

Shared Financial Picture

Each partner enters income — approximate is fine. Commitments are amortised, including quarterly and annual expenses, into a true monthly equivalent, so the dashboard reflects what you actually need to set aside each month. A net worth view rounds out the picture: what you own against what you owe.

Shared Goals

Savings goals, debt payoff goals, and gap reduction goals — each with a target, a monthly contribution, and a projected completion date calculated from your real history, not just the number you typed in.

Monthly Check-in & History

The deliberate close of each month: both partners review what happened, compare it to the plan, and agree on the path forward. Completing it records a financial snapshot — building a longitudinal record you can look back on.

Built for Jamaica & the Caribbean

Multi-currency (JMD + USD) from day one. Informal debt captured explicitly as a liability type. No bank connection required — a real fit for a market where open banking simply isn't there yet.

Design Principles

What guides the product

01

Radical Transparency Between Partners

Financial alignment only works when both partners are fully open. No private fields, no separate views, no hidden balances.

02

Alignment Over Automation

The goal isn't to automate your finances but to align your household. The value comes from shared understanding, not data accuracy.

03

Planning Over Tracking

Common Ground focuses on where you're going, not where every dollar went — high-level, long-term thinking over day-to-day monitoring.

04

The Household Is Two People, Not One

Most finance apps treat a household as a single entity. Common Ground acknowledges it's two people with shared goals and individual contexts — and designs for that relationship dynamic.

05

Family Inclusion, Age-Appropriately

Children can be given a curated, read-only view of selected family goals — building financial literacy naturally without exposing the full household picture.

06

Meet Households Where They Are

Surplus or deficit, debt-free or debt-laden — the app adapts to your reality without judgment. Progress is progress, wherever you're starting from.

07

Financial Health Is a Story, Not a Number

A net worth figure alone doesn't tell you if a household is in trouble or on track. Common Ground helps households understand where they stand, where they're headed, and how they feel about it.
