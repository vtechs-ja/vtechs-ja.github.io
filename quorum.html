The Problem

Run with notebooks, WhatsApp, and spreadsheets

Jamaica has thousands of sports clubs, civic associations, community groups, and PTAs that hold real communities together. The people who run them — secretaries, treasurers, club managers — do so with notebooks, WhatsApp groups, and spreadsheets. There's no lightweight, affordable tool built for this context that handles the full administrative picture: members, subgroups, dues, events, attendance, resources, and communications — all in one place.

What Quorum Does

Everything a group manager needs

A configurable feature set means every group only sees what's relevant to them — sports clubs, civic committees, and volunteer organisations all run on the same platform, shaped to fit.

Members & Subgroups

A searchable member roster with subgroups for teams, age groups, cohorts, or committees — including annual rollover for cycle-based groups.

Events & Attendance

A mobile-first field screen for marking attendance at training grounds and meetings — one-tap, optimistic saves, built for spotty connections.

Dues Management

Set a dues schedule, record payments, and see at a glance who's paid, who's partial, and who's outstanding — no more chasing a spreadsheet.

Training Logs

Track training delivered to members or subgroups — trainer, topic, duration — useful for sponsor and NGO reporting.

Resource Tracking

Log kit and equipment issued to members, track returns, and see outstanding items sorted overdue-first.

Reports

Attendance rates, hours logged, and dues collection — a report you can hand to the chairman without spending a Sunday compiling it.

Who It's For

Built for the administrator

Sports Clubs

Football clubs, netball associations, cricket clubs, and track & field teams — member registration, training attendance, subgroups by age or team, and kit tracking.

Civic & Community Groups

Design Principles

What guides the product

01

Built for the Administrator

The primary user is the person doing the operational work — the secretary, treasurer, or group manager. Every design decision optimises for their experience first.

02

Invisible Configurability

Every group is different. Features can be enabled or disabled per group during setup — members never see toggles or irrelevant sections.

03

No Group Left Behind

The free tier must be genuinely useful, not a teaser. Small community groups with no budget should be able to run their organisation properly without paying.

04

Amplify What Already Works

These groups function today through trust, relationships, and informal systems. Quorum digitises the administration without replacing the human fabric that makes communities work.

05

Continuity Over Dependency

A group shouldn't grind to a halt because one person is unavailable. Multi-admin access ensures operational continuity and distributes responsibility appropriately.

06

Start Where Groups Are

Members in most Jamaican community groups have no expectation of a digital portal. Quorum meets groups where they are and adds member-facing access progressively as trust and adoption grow.

07

Field-First on Mobile

Screens used in the field — attendance, hours logging, payment recording — are mobile-first: large tap targets, minimal typing, one action per screen.
