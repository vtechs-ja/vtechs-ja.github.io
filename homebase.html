The Problem

Running a home is a team effort — most tools forget that

Most households lack a shared operating system, so the mental load falls disproportionately on one person. Tasks get missed not because people don't want to contribute, but because there's no shared picture of what needs doing, who's doing it, and whether the home is covered. Existing apps treat this as a motivation problem, rewarding task completion with points and badges — but the real problem is clarity and shared accountability, not motivation. Most of them are also built for parents to manage children, not for a household to operate as a genuine team.

What HomeBase Does

Clarity, not motivation

HomeBase assumes people want to contribute — its job is to make it clear what needs doing, who is doing it, and whether the home is covered.

Task Library

Household tasks organised by area, with a starter library of 30 common tasks — and support for multi-instance tasks like per-bathroom cleaning.

Rhythms, Not Deadlines

Slot-based, window-based, and one-off scheduling models household work the way it actually happens — recurring and cyclical, not a to-do list.

Weekly Check-in

A deliberate weekly ritual that generates the coming week's tasks, captures how the week went, and anchors a real family conversation.

Shared Household View

Everyone sees the full picture — status-first, then by member — with personal tasks anchored at the top.

Assignment by Capability

Tasks assigned based on what each member can actually do, with capability notes on every member's profile.

Supply Management

Track what's running low, scan barcodes while restocking, and step through a guided "I went shopping" flow that covers the whole shop run.

Design Principles

What guides the product

01

The Household Is a Team, Not a Hierarchy

Every member contributes within their capability. HomeBase doesn't position parents as managers and children as workers — everyone is a participant in a shared home.

02

Clarity Over Motivation

The app doesn't try to make people want to contribute — it assumes they do. Its job is to make the picture clear.

03

The Home Runs on Rhythm, Not Deadlines

Household work is recurring and cyclical. The system models tasks as rhythms and windows, not due dates.

04

Show What Happened. Never Say What It Means.

Contribution is made visible, not judged. Interpretation belongs to the people who live together, not to the app.

05

Infrastructure, Not Intervention

The app is a system that runs quietly in the background. No push, no nag, no gamification.

06

Respect Existing Communication

Households already have ways of talking to each other. HomeBase handles what those channels cannot: shared memory, structure, and visibility.

07

Built for Adults, Honest with Children

The primary audience is adults who want a well-run home and are willing to have real conversations with their children about contribution.

08

Utility-First, Not Social

Any shared or community features serve a utility purpose. HomeBase does not build a social graph, feed, or engagement loop.
