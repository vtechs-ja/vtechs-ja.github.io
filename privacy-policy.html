Plain-language summary: We collect only what we need to provide our services. We do not sell your personal data. We keep your data secure and respect your rights under applicable law, including Jamaica’s Data Protection Act 2020.

1. Who We Are

Vortex Technologies Limited (“Vortex Technologies,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is a technology company incorporated in Jamaica. We design, build, and operate software products and deliver technology services to individuals and businesses.

For the purposes of data protection law, Vortex Technologies Limited is the data controller responsible for the personal data processed under this policy.

Contact: info@vtechs.com.jm

2. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • The Vortex Technologies corporate website at vtechs.com.jm
  • All software products developed and operated by Vortex Technologies (including Quorum, Common Ground, HomeBase, Learnaz, FinCore, Conductor, and OnTrack)
  • Any services, APIs, or platforms we provide to clients or partners

Individual products may publish supplemental privacy notices that address product-specific data practices. Where a supplemental notice exists, it applies in addition to this policy. In the event of a conflict, the supplemental notice takes precedence for that product.

3. Data We Collect

3.1 Data You Provide Directly

  • Account information: name, email address, username, and password when you register for one of our products.
  • Profile data: any additional information you choose to add to your profile (e.g., organisation name, role, profile photo).
  • Communications: messages you send us via contact forms, email, or in-app support channels.
  • Payment information: billing address and transaction records. Card numbers are processed directly by our payment processor and are not stored on our systems.
  • User-generated content: content you create, upload, or share within our products.
  • Financial information: income sources, monthly commitments, assets, liabilities, debts, and financial goals you enter within Common Ground. This data is stored at the household level and is visible to all members of your household.
  • Household coordination data (HomeBase): household member profiles (including display name, household role, and optional capability notes), task and assignment records, task completion history (including who completed a task and when), weekly check-in records (including qualitative sentiment and optional notes), and per-member notification preferences.

Household member data — Common Ground: within Common Ground, users are paired into a shared household. Both members of a household have full visibility of all financial data entered by either member. This is a core product feature, not incidental sharing.

Children's profiles — HomeBase: HomeBase supports child members via profile-only accounts, which are created and managed by a parent or household admin. Profile-only accounts do not require an email address and are secured by a parent-set 4-digit passcode. Only a display name and household role are collected for profile-only members. No independent account is created for the child.

3.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, actions taken, session duration, and interaction patterns.
  • Device & technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device identifiers, and screen resolution.
  • Log data: server logs including timestamps, request paths, and error events.
  • Push notification tokens: device tokens used to deliver in-app notifications, such as monthly check-in reminders.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: see Section 11 for details.

3.3 Data from Third Parties

  • Authentication providers: if you sign in via a third-party identity provider (e.g., Google, Apple), we receive profile data that provider shares with us (typically name and email address).
  • Analytics and error-reporting services: aggregated or pseudonymous data about product usage and performance.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use personal data for the following purposes:

  • Providing and operating our products and services — account management, authentication, feature delivery, and customer support.
  • Improving our products — analysing usage patterns to fix bugs, optimise performance, and prioritise new features.
  • Communications — sending transactional messages (e.g., account verification, password reset), service updates, and where you have opted in, product news and updates.
  • Security and fraud prevention — detecting, investigating, and preventing unauthorised access, abuse, or violations of our terms.
  • Legal compliance — meeting obligations under applicable laws and responding to lawful requests from authorities.
  • Business operations — billing, invoicing, and record-keeping.

We do not use financial data entered in Common Ground for any purpose beyond delivering the product to your household. We do not benchmark, analyse, or aggregate household financial data for any commercial purpose.

We do not use your personal data for automated individual decision-making that produces significant legal or similarly significant effects without your explicit consent.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under Jamaica’s Data Protection Act 2020 and, where applicable, equivalent legislation in other jurisdictions, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance: processing necessary to fulfil a contract with you (e.g., providing a product you have signed up for).
  • Legitimate interests: processing necessary for our legitimate business interests, such as product improvement, security, and fraud prevention, where these interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligation: processing required to comply with applicable law.
  • Consent: for optional communications and non-essential cookies, where we ask for your consent and you provide it. You may withdraw consent at any time.

6. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share data only in the following circumstances:

6.1 Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party service providers to help operate our products. These include cloud hosting providers, payment processors, analytics platforms, and customer support tools. Providers are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

6.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose data if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal request (e.g., a court order or subpoena), or if we believe disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, prevent fraud, or ensure the safety of users or the public.

6.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or part of our business, personal data may be transferred to the acquiring entity. We will provide notice before data becomes subject to a materially different privacy policy.

6.4 Aggregate or De-identified Data

We may share aggregated or de-identified data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you, for purposes such as industry reporting or research.

7. International Data Transfers

Vortex Technologies operates from Jamaica. Our service providers may process data in other countries, including the United States and European Union member states. Where data is transferred outside Jamaica, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as standard contractual clauses or equivalent mechanisms recognised under applicable law.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. Typical retention periods:

  • Account data: retained for the life of your account, plus a short period thereafter to allow for account reactivation or dispute resolution.
  • Transaction records: retained for a minimum of seven years in accordance with applicable financial record-keeping requirements.
  • Support communications: retained for up to three years after the issue is resolved.
  • Usage and log data: typically retained for 90 days, unless required for longer for security or legal purposes.

When data is no longer required, we delete or anonymise it securely.

9. How We Protect Your Data

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These include encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular security reviews. For a full description of our security practices, please refer to our Security Policy.

No transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected.
  • Erasure: request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations. You can submit a deletion request via our Data Deletion Request page.
  • Restriction: request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection: object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@vtechs.com.jm. We will respond within 30 days. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request.

If you are unsatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction.

11. Cookies & Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate our products and understand how they are used.

Types of cookies we use

  • Essential cookies: required for the product to function (e.g., session management, authentication). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: help us understand how users interact with our products so we can improve them. We use privacy-respecting analytics tools and, where possible, anonymise IP addresses.
  • Preference cookies: remember your settings and preferences between visits.

You can control non-essential cookies through your browser settings or our cookie preferences tool (where available). Disabling certain cookies may affect product functionality.

12. Children’s Privacy

Our corporate website and most of our services are not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent.

Some of our products may include minor users. Learnaz is designed for educational settings that may include minors. HomeBase is designed for households that include children as active participants; child members use profile-only accounts that are created and managed by a parent or household admin, with no independent account or email address required (see Section 3.1). Where a product involves minor users, we implement safeguards appropriate for child users and, where required by applicable law, parental consent mechanisms.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child under 13 without appropriate consent, please contact us at info@vtechs.com.jm and we will delete it promptly.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last Updated” date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you via email or a prominent notice within our products.

Your continued use of our products after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
